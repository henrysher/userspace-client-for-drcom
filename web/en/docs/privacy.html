The Drcom-Client Open Source Project

Privacy Policy

Statement and Purpose:


drcom-client is committed to respecting developers and volunteers right to privacy. As an team, we recognize the need for appropriate protection and management of any personally identifiable information provided to us.

drcom-client has established this privacy policy to let developers and volunteers know that any personal and/or identifiable information under drcom-client’s care will be protected. drcom-client intends to develop proper measures to ensure that information under the team’s care is protected when using drcom-client’s electronic devices. drcom-client is committed to ensuring compliance with applicable privacy legislation.

Implementation:

Disclosure of Information to Third Parties


The only circumstances under which personal information may be disclosed to third parties is for the fulfillment of any purposes identified above, or as required by law. If personal information is disclosed to third parties for the fulfillment of any purposes identified above, drcom-client will ensure that appropriate security undertakings, such as confidentiality clauses in contractual agreements are employed to protect the transfer and use of personal information. drcom-client does not sell, trade, or rent information to third parties.

Privacy when using this Website


E-mail sent to this website is kept strictly confidential. Your e-mail address will not be sold or shared with anyone outside of drcom-client.


drcom-client does not use your visits to this website to collect your personal information. drcom-client tracks the number of visitors to its websites for statistical purposes and to help it improve services. The type of data tracked includes pages opened, length of visit, type of browser used, and — possibly — the city or country where the browser is located.

Mailing List Policy

(This applies to User list, Mailing List.)


These are public mailing lists. When you send a message to any of these addresses, it will be forwarded to all the list subscribers, including drcom-client developers and others. Google Groups and other web sites may publish archives of these lists.


Your e-mail address will always be truncated in published archives for your privacy, and the drcom-client developers will not share your address with anyone. However, we cannot prevent other subscribers from seeing or publishing your messages. Therefore we recommend you avoid putting contact details such as street addresses or telephone numbers in your e-mails.

Concerns or questions regarding drcom-client


If you have any questions about this policy or are not comfortable writing to a public mailing list, please send a private e-mail to our lead developer.
